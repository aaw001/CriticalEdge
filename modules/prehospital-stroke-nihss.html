Prehospital Stroke & NIHSS

Why It Matters

Every minute counts in stroke. Neurons die rapidly; delays translate into permanent disability. The prehospital team’s role is recognition, rapid triage, and preparation for reperfusion therapy.

Time Goal: Transport should begin within 25 minutes of first medical contact.

Primary Actions

  • Primary/secondary survey
  • Identify onset / last-known-normal
  • Vitals: SpO₂, RR, HR, BP, glucose, temperature
  • O₂ if SpO₂ < 90% (target 94–96%; 88–92% if COPD)
  • Check anticoagulation/bleed risks
  • FAST‑VAN → NIHSS as indicated
  • Prealert + data transmission
FAST‑VAN → Suspected stroke?
Establish onset (or wake‑up stroke)
Red flags for ICH / anticoagulation
Pathway: Lysis <4.5h • EVT up to 24h
Go to stroke/EVT center

Supportive Measures

  • Head 30° (flat if severe carotid stenosis + flow‑dependent neuro)
  • IV access (18G above wrist; right side preferred)
  • Crystalloid as needed; treat fever >37.5 °C; keep glucose 6–10 mmol/L
  • Consider BP control if SBP >220 mmHg; treat seizures (e.g., midazolam)
Remember: ~15% are hemorrhages — CT is required before treatment.

Recognition Tools

PSL (Prate, Smile, Løfte) — a FAST variant:

  • P – Prate: Dysarthria/language disturbance
  • S – Smile: Facial asymmetry
  • L – Løfte: Arm/leg weakness

TIAs may fully resolve but signal high short-term stroke risk — always convey to a stroke center.

NIHSS (Collapsible Reference)

1. Level of Consciousness (LOC) — 0–3
  • 0 Alert
  • 1 Not alert; arousable
  • 2 Needs repeated/painful stimulation
  • 3 Unresponsive
1a. LOC Questions — 0–2
  • 0 Both correct
  • 1 One correct
  • 2 Neither
1b. LOC Commands — 0–2
  • 0 Both
  • 1 One
  • 2 Neither
2. Best Gaze — 0–2
  • 0 Normal
  • 1 Partial palsy
  • 2 Forced deviation
3. Visual Fields — 0–3
  • 0 Normal
  • 1 Partial hemi
  • 2 Complete hemi
  • 3 Bilateral
4. Facial Palsy — 0–3
  • 0 Normal
  • 1 Minor
  • 2 Partial
  • 3 Complete
5. Motor Arm (each) — 0–4
  • 0 No drift (10 s)
  • 1 Drift
  • 2 Some effort
  • 3 No effort vs gravity
  • 4 No movement
6. Motor Leg (each) — 0–4
  • 0 No drift (5 s)
  • 1 Drift
  • 2 Some effort
  • 3 No effort vs gravity
  • 4 No movement
7. Limb Ataxia — 0–2
  • 0 Absent
  • 1 One limb
  • 2 Two limbs

Only score if truly ataxic (not weak).

8. Sensory — 0–2
  • 0 Normal
  • 1 Mild–mod loss
  • 2 Severe/total loss
9. Best Language — 0–3
  • 0 No aphasia
  • 1 Mild–mod
  • 2 Severe
  • 3 Mute/global
10. Dysarthria — 0–2
  • 0 Normal
  • 1 Mild–mod
  • 2 Severe
11. Extinction & Inattention — 0–2
  • 0 None
  • 1 One modality
  • 2 Multimodal/profound
Destination cue: NIHSS high (≈≥10) or VAN+ → consider direct thrombectomy center if regional delay vs nearest ED is < 30–60 min.

Brain Physiology Snapshot

The brain is ~2% of body weight but uses ~20% of O₂ and ~25% of glucose. CBF autoregulates roughly MAP 60–160 mmHg; below ~20 ml/100 g/min ischemia begins — hence the sprint mentality in stroke.
